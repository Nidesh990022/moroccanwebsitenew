Deep Tissue Massage

A traditional massage is a deep, full-body treatment that begins at the feet and ends at the head. Using a sequence of gentle, flowing exercise movements, the recipient’s body is moved, loosened and stretched in order to increase muscle flexibility and joint range of motion. Massage employs stretching, pulling, and rocking techniques.

Swedish Massage

Swedish massage is one of the most commonly offered massage techniques. It’s sometimes called a classic massage. The technique aims to promote relaxation by releasing muscle tension. Swedish massage is gentler than deep tissue massage and better suited for people interested in relaxation and tension relief.

Moroccan Special

The Moroccan Special Therapy is an excellent way to nourish, exfoliate and encourage skin cell regeneration. The scrub used for the therapy is a nourishing exfoliate full of minerals that is good for rubbing and soaking your skin. It is a gentle exfoliate perfect for encouraging cell renewal and adding that youthful glow.

Relaxation Massage

Relaxation massage is a therapeutic technique designed to promote overall well-being by easing tension and stress in the body and mind. Often referred to as Swedish massage, it uses long, flowing strokes, kneading, and circular movements to increase blood circulation, reduce muscle tension, and enhance relaxation.

Kerala Ayurveda Massage

Kerala Ayurveda Massage is a traditional therapeutic treatment from India that combines herbal oils, gentle strokes, and deep-tissue techniques to balance the body’s energy. This massage enhances circulation, detoxifies, and promotes relaxation. Rooted in ancient Ayurvedic principles.

Steam Bath

A steam bath is a therapeutic treatment that involves inhaling hot, moist air to promote relaxation and improve circulation. The heat opens pores, helping detoxify the skin and relieve muscle tension. Regular use can enhance respiratory health, improve skin tone, and reduce stress, offering a soothing, rejuvenating experience.
